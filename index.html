About Me

I am a second-year undergraduate student in the Department of Statistics at Renmin University of China (RUC), majoring in Data Science and Big Data Technology (Class of 2024). My academic record places me in the top 5% of my cohort, with a GPA of 3.9.

My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, urban computing, and natural language processing. I have been involved in projects spanning spatio-temporal flow prediction and controllable affective text generation, with work published at or submitted to top venues (WWW 2026, ACL 2026).

Beyond research, I serve as Head of the Theory Department in the Party Building Promotion Committee at the School of Statistics, and have accumulated over 130 hours of volunteer service. I am also an enthusiast of competitive mathematics — having won the Third Prize (National Level) in the National Mathematics Competition for College Students.

Research Experience

WED-Net: Weather-Effect Disentanglement for Urban Flow Prediction
Advisor: Prof. Xiao Zhou  ·  Renmin University of China  ·  2025 – 2026

Proposed a novel disentanglement network that separates weather-induced effects from intrinsic urban flow patterns using causal augmentation. The model significantly improves generalization under adverse weather conditions. The work was accepted at WWW 2026.

Controllable Affective Generation via Latent Vector Steering
Advisor: Prof. Xiao Zhou  ·  Renmin University of China  ·  2025 – 2026

Investigated latent-space steering techniques for fine-grained control of affective attributes in generated text without task-specific fine-tuning. The paper is currently under review at ACL 2026.

Academic Quality in the AI Era: LLM-Assisted Writing Detection
